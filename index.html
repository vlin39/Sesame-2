Part 1:

Sketch and Wireframe

Visualizing our Ideas

For this project, 4 of us, Allen Dufort, Sofia Guarisma, Kaley Newlin, and Valentina Lin worked in a team to bring a company's idea to life. We had the oppertunity to work with "Sesame" for this project. The company "Sesame" works to get healthcare to users faster and cheaper while retaining high quality.

Our first step in working with Sesame was to understand what problems they wanted to tackle with our new design, as well as to brainstorm. The main points we has to tackle were:

  • Creating personalized and engaging webpages
  • Provide the user with enhanced understanding and personalized learning
  • Accessibility and clarity, making sure information is easy to understand and the website is easy to use.

We then each sketched out what we envisioned the product to be.

After sketching our ideas, we talked about how we can best tackle the design. We made wireframes integrating our ideas together using a software called "Balsamiq"

When making design decisions, we wanted to ensure that the usability of the website was high and that patients could customize their healthcare. One component we kept from two of the sketches was a simple Welcome Page to allow users to easily access different features/pages across Sesame’s website. In our opinion, this would allow for users to have greater clarity on what tools were available to them in the interface. Another component that we kept was a Profile Page where users can look at their specific health stats, along with personalized article/educational module recommendations and suggested actions. We believe this decision allows for greater accessibility and patient empowerment, as it makes it easier for users to learn about aspects of their health that they may be concerned about and what they can take into their own hands to address them. Every sketch had some sort of screen that gave users personalized health tips and advice, so we combined that into an educational Health Tip Page. This would allow for less overwhelming information, as one page would provide all the information users need about a certain topic, condition, or medication they’re researching. We also decided to create a separate Symptoms Page, found in one of our sketches, so users could ask for consultations based on health symptoms they had.

We ultimately decided to develop four different web pages: a Welcome Page, a Personal Profile Page, a Symptoms Page, and a Health Tip page. We wanted the users to be able to easily navigate and use the website, while also being able to get specialized care. The designs, specifically the Health Tip page, include features incorporating AI to increase accessibility and lessen gaps in healthcare language and knowledge for users.
